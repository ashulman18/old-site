Project 1

How Can We Best Utilize Experts?

An audiologists’ experience is invaluable, especially without an ethnography. We had access to a very busy few at Bose.

I was given a list of questions to start out with. Over the course of onboarding, researching, attending meetings, etc., I added 25 more questions to our list.

These were in a spreadsheet with the following columns:
Questions, Use Scenario, Question Origin, More Info

Then, I split these into 5 workstreams for various teams to tackle since not all questions required experts.

To optimize our time with experts (audiologists) I determined:

Experts can provide information about:

  • Surface-level generalizations regarding users, their questions, expectations, and mental models
  • Current state of the art, everyday theories
  • General mistakes people have made in the past

Experts should be consulted for:

  • Purchase journey (e.g. emotions during diagnosis)
  • Considering edge cases
  • Persona/needs feedback
  • Marketing feedback
