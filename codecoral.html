Role + Team

On a team of three Design Impact (M.S.) students, I engaged in weekly hospital visits for interviewing and testing, mocked up medium fidelity prototypes for rapid iteration, explored design exercises, collaborated on the website and branding, and engaged advisors across fields.

Challenge

"In medicine, a good day means meeting your kindergarten needs of sleep, bathroom, and food."
Half of physicians report at least one symptom of burnout. Burnout, now a medical condition, leads to medical error, which is the third leading cause of death in America.

Outcome

Coral Connect connects doctors to resources their hospitals already provide in a smart, timely, and quick manner. It builds trust with doctors, breaking down their barriers to reaching out for support.

Overview

Given

When I joined the team, they had already determined archetypes and six simple prototypes as jumping off points from some user research.

Empathize + Define

Insights

Through hundreds of hours of facetime with physicians and more doing design exercises around them, we found the following insights:

  • Physician burnout is a cycle
  • “It’ll go on my record”
  • Mental wellbeing is not urgent
  • Work-family balance is onerous
  • “I know what the therapist will say”

Initial  Direction

After testing, we pursued our “Coral Board” prototype.

  • Ability to monitor physical and emotional metrics
  • Timely notifications encouraging helpful bite-sized actions
  • Resources to offload their tasks and/or get support

Ultimate Coral Board prototype


Findings: Seeing data increased guilt, but resources inspired “wow!”

Resources were now front and center, with personal data visualization secondary.

Concluding  Remarks
