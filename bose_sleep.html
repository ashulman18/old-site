    Summer 2019 - 11 weeks

Wellington, FL

Role + Team

I worked mostly independently, with advice, insights, and direction from a UX Designer in Sleep.

Challenge

Determine user interactions for new features for Bose's new sleepbuds given predetermined solutions and needs.

Outcome

I ran a workshop with the full team to provide and discuss recommendations and future steps for the product.

Define

Given

I was given a series of images like the one below. There were numerous ‘themes’ or sections with problems and possible broad solutions and supporting capabilities


Empathize

User Information Deep Dive

To start, I went through as much user information Bose collected as possible, filling in any gaps with online anecdotes and expertise. Starting with what the company already collected allowed me to better understand both the project scope and the target user. Usually, I get my user research from in-person interviews but without the resources to do this, I turned to the web.

Define + Ideate

Structuring Ideation

I then needed to determine the user interactions for each proposed solution in an organized manner. This was a process and taught me the value of spreadsheets and keeping track of various related, simultaneous brainstorms.

Ultimately, I determined that a spreadsheet would be best for organization and ideation.

Behavior Design

Mapping Interactions

After working on a project with Behavior Design expert BJ Fogg, I was equipped with skills to help people change behaviors and habits. This experience deeply aided me in this project because many of these interactions required repetitive engagement to ensure efficacy.

Ideate

Exploration

I brainstormed many user interactions for the various problems and proposed solutions, added columns for feasibility, barriers to use of each feature, and much more.

Finding examples of specific interactions was taxing; empathizing through user stories jump-started my interest.

Overview

Business  Shifts

The product definition shifted away from hardware, and, in turn, I highlighted the risk of the loss in the spreadsheet.

Overview

Workshop

Throughout the process, I generated many questions. The team prioritized these over user interaction work as answers would help us align on overall goals and vision of the project.
We needed to take a step back.

Overview

Favorite Lessons

  • Empathy for my users drives my motivation
  • Changes happen early and often: assess risk and remember goals
  • Priorities can shift a lot, at least in the early stage of a product
  • Trying various methods results in deeper understanding
